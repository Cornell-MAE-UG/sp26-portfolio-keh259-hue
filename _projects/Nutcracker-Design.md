Given: 
    Avergae size of a macadamia nut: 20 mm
    Average max grip strenght: 50.7kg
    Average load to crack a nut: 222.18kg

Find:
    Design to make this feasible


Design:
    Lever style nutcracker with a simple pivot and two handles.
    The nut rests in a small, semi-circular cavity at the pivot end.
    The handles act as levers. The force at the ends of the handles is multiplied at the pivot to crack the nut.
    The pivot is a simple pin.

Calculations:
    Net moment about the pivot = 0 --> solving for when Fout = Fcrack
    Fout(0.02) - Fgrip(x) = 0
    497.2x = 0.02(2178.84)
    x = 0.088 m

    Mechanical advantage = Fout/Fin = 4.38


Usability: 
    This design is usable because the mechanical advantage of 4.38 allows enough force to crack the nut with a normal grip strength. However, the handle length of 0.088m is fairly short, which could make it harder to holf comfortably. Overall, the design should work in theory, but a longer handle would make it easier to use.
